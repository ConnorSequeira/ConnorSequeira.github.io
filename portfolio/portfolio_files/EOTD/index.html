End of Tooling Design

Connor Sequeira

Next

Goals

Addressing the Breakage of the Existing Design

The main goal of this project was to address the observed breakage of the preexisting tooling design and create a more robust, durable solution. The failure of the original design highlighted the need for a thorough evaluation and redesign, ensuring that the tooling could withstand the stresses and operational demands of the pneumatic activated magnetic gripper. Using FEA software (SimScale), the objective was to assess the structural integrity of the original tooling and identify areas for improvement, ultimately leading to a stronger and more reliable design.

Next

Gripper Design Iterations

Designing for Strength and Magnetism Adjustment

Multiple gripper endpiece designs were created to better hold the cylindrical metal parts while improving magnetism. The design iterations explored different shapes and materials to adjust the distance between the magnet and the part, thereby improving the strength of the grip. Material selection played a crucial role in optimizing the magnetic field, ensuring the gripper's functionality was aligned with the production requirements.

Additionally, the designs were optimized for 3D printing to ensure quick, durable, and support-free printing. Features like streamlined shapes and reinforced sections were included to prevent breakage and ensure the tooling could be manufactured easily while maintaining its strength and longevity in the high-demand operational environment.

Next

Results

Improved Durability, Faster Production, and Less Down Time

The final tooling design proved to be much more robust and efficient than the original. By using a lofted boss in the 3D CAD model, the new design printed faster and was significantly more durable. The original tooling would break after just two hours of use, but the new design has yet to break after extensive testing. FEA simulations confirmed the improvements in the design's structural integrity, and the updated tooling now better meets the needs of the pneumatic activated magnetic gripper, offering both reliability and longevity in production.
